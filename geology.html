the mariana trench is the subduction zone between the pacific and philippine tectonic plates.

as the philippine plate moves in a northeasterly direction, the pacific plate does the same. when they meet, the pacific plate "subducts," or pushes under the philippine plate. this causes the trench to form and deepen.

geological material at the western portion of the pacific plate is some of the oldest oceanic crust on earth.
